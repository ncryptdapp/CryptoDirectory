Navigate the digital asset landscape by understanding the fundamental distinction between native blockchain Coins and platform-based Tokens.

Coins

Coins are the native cryptocurrencies of their own independent blockchain. They are used to pay for transaction fees, secure the network through staking or mining, and often serve as the primary medium of exchange within their ecosystem. They are fundamental to the blockchain's operation.

Key Examples:

  • Bitcoin (BTC): The pioneer cryptocurrency, digital gold.
  • Ethereum (ETH): Powers the leading smart contract platform.
  • Solana (SOL): Known for its high transaction speed and low costs.
  • Cardano (ADA): A research-driven proof-of-stake blockchain.

Tokens

Tokens are digital assets built on an existing blockchain platform, rather than having their own. They can represent a wide range of utilities, assets, or even voting rights within a decentralized application (dApp). They leverage the security and infrastructure of their host blockchain.

Key Examples:

  • Tether (USDT): A stablecoin pegged to the U.S. Dollar.
  • Chainlink (LINK): Decentralized oracle network for smart contracts.
  • Uniswap (UNI): Governance token for the popular DEX.
  • Decentraland (MANA): Utility token for a virtual reality platform.
